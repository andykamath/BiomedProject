Healthy eating is an important part of managing your type 2 diabetes. You may not always have to follow a special menu, but you should try to make smart decisions about what you eat.

  • Should eat a balance of fruits, vegetables, lean meats (like poultry and fish), and beans for protein
  • Balance the number of calories you eat with your activity level
  • Choose foods rich in whole grains, vegetables, and fruits
  • Limit your intake of saturated fats, cholesterol, salt, and added sugar
  • Typically diabetics should consume starchy foods that contain complex sugars
  • Complex sugars are harder to break down and therefore provide the body with a source of sugar over a longer period of time.
  • Diabetes makes you more likely to get heart disease. So limiting fat, especially saturated fat and artificial Tran’s fats, is key.
