  • What is Type II Diabetes

    Type II diabetes is the most common form of diabtes. If someone has Type II diabetes their body creates, but dosent use insuin properly, this is also known as insulin ressistance. Insulin is a hormone that is secereted by the pancrease that allows body cells such as liver cells to absorb glucose from the blood. When Type II diabtes first starts to develop your pancreas makes extra insulin to make up for it, but over time it isn't able to keep up and can't make enough insulin. This results in a increase of blood sugar since the glucose in the blood cannot be absorbed properly.

  • What are the drawbacks of High blood Sugar?

    Over time, the high glucose levels in the blood can damage the nerves and small blood vessels of the eyes, kidneys, and heart and lead to atherosclerosis, or hardening of the arteries that can cause heart attack and stroke. The buildup of sugar in the blood can also cause an increase in urination, causing dehydration.

    Source

  • Who gets Type II diabetes?

    Anyone can get type 2 diabetes, but those at highest risk for the disease are those who: are over 45, are obese or overweight, have family members who have type 2 diabetes, have prediabetes, don't exercise, have low HDL cholesterol or high triglycerides, and have high blood pressure.
